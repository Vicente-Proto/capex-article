Estimating Maintenance CapEx

Author: Venkat Peddireddy

Institution: China Europe International Business School (CEIBS)

Date: September 2021

Abstract

Technological obsolescence significantly impacts the economic life of long-term assets. This paper introduces an innovative measure of maintenance CapEx, accounting for traditional depreciation and technological obsolescence. Findings suggest that firms typically underestimate depreciation, leading to future earnings impairments and negative abnormal stock returns, highlighting the importance of accurately measuring maintenance capital expenditures.

Sections

  • 1. Introduction: Importance of accurately measuring capacity costs.
  • 2. Background and Prior Literature: Existing depreciation methods and their limitations.
  • 3. Methodology: Model for estimating maintenance CapEx.
  • 4. Data and Sample Statistics: Sample description and descriptive statistics.
  • 5. Results: Analysis of under-depreciation and implications for future earnings.
  • 6. Stock Return Analysis: Relationship between under-depreciation and future abnormal returns.
  • 7. Investment Implications: Understanding investment decisions using the new maintenance CapEx measure.
  • 8. Conclusion: Implications of findings for financial reporting and investment practices.
